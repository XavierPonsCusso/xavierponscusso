ESTROPAJO

#ProductDesign

#ArduinoSynth

Estropajo is a personal project that fuses music, electronics, and product design. Developed in collaboration with designer Sara López Morales (parametrip.com), this project aims to explore the creative potential of Arduino, basic electronic components, and music. By utilizing the Mozzi Library, Estropajo transforms an Arduino into a versatile musical instrument, simulating a simple oscillator with adjustable pitch and sound wave. The project features stereo audio output, ready to be connected to speakers, all housed inside a playful and used kitchen scourer.
The project is controlled by two potentiometers that adjust the sound wave, producing a unique and customizable audio experience. The stereo audio jack 3.5 output delivers a high-quality sound that is perfect for use with speakers.

What makes Estropajo stand out is its unique and playful design. By incorporating all of the electronic components inside a kitchen scourer, it adds a humorous touch to the project while also making it a fun conversation starter. This project demonstrates my passion for exploring new possibilities in the intersection of technology, design, and music.
