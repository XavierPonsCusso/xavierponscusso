SONOGRAPHY

#ProductDesign

#AnalogPhotography

#Electronics


Sonography is a personal project born from the concept of synesthesia , which translates
sounds into unique images
using basic Arduino electronic components and analog photography techniques. By combining two seemingly disparate themes and utilizing digital and analog technology, Sonography produces a distinct output that engages the senses.

The product captures and processes the frequencies emitted by an integrated microphone, dividing them into eight different channels from bass to treble. Each time a channel detects a decibel level that exceeds a predetermined limit, it activates one of the eight servos. Each servo opens a small 3D printed sliding door, allowing light to pass through a tiny hole and project onto photosensitive photographic paper. Meanwhile, a stepper motor holds a rotatory platform where the sensitive paper is placed. The result is a circular pattern on the photographic paper that, after being developed in a darkroom lab, becomes a visual representation of sound.

Sonography's versatility allows it to convert any sound into a unique image, whether it's a favorite song or a conversation with someone special. Created in April 2021,
this project highlights the potential of combining two vastly different fields
(music and analog photography) to create something truly original and engaging.
